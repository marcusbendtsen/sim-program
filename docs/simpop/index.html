Population dynamics

SIMPOP

In this project, we will use Swedish national registries to define the initial state of the virtual cohorts and estimate conditional probabilities of life course events. These life course events relate to education, household characteristics, employment, income trajectories, sick leave, and sickness- and rehabilitation benefits.

In what follows, we use the word characteristics to mean an individual’s: age, sex, birth country, biological and non-biological parents’ birth country, educational level, disposable income, (un)employment, retirement, household characteristics, and marital status.

Estimands

Household characteristics

  • Annual probability of two individuals getting married, conditional on both individuals’ characteristics.
  • Annual probability of two married individuals getting divorced, conditional on both individuals’ characteristics.
  • Annual probability of adding a child to the household, conditional on parent(‘s’) characteristics.
  • Annual probability of child leaving the household, conditional on the child's and parent(‘s’) characteristics.

Education and employment

  • Annual probability of obtaining an educational degree, conditional on the individual’s and parents’ characteristics.
  • Annual probability of becoming employed (or changing employment), conditional on the individual’s and the family’s characteristics.
  • Annual probability of becoming unemployed, conditional on the individual’s and the family’s characteristics.
  • Annual probability of retirement, conditional on the individual’s and the family’s characteristics.

Sick leave and benefits

Note that a complete specification of relevant ICD-10 codes for the below two estimands can be found at the end of this document.

  • Number of days of sickness and rehabilitation benefits due to a specific non-communicable disease or mental health disorder, conditional on the individual’s characteristics.
  • Amount paid (sickness and rehabilitation benefits) and case management costs due to a specific non-communicable disease or mental health disorder, conditional on the individual’s characteristics.

Income trajectories

>

Predicted income trajectory conditional on the individual’s and the family’s characteristics and on history of days with sickness and rehabilitation benefits.
