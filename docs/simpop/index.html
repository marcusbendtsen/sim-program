Population dynamics

SIMPOP

In this project, we will use Swedish national registries to define the initial state of the virtual cohorts and estimate conditional probabilities of life course events. These life course events relate to household characteristics, education, employment, income trajectories, and sick leave.

"Individual characteristics" is broadly defined as what is know about an individual, e.g. age, gender, education, current income, household characteristics, NCDs, MHCs, history of sick leave, etc.

Household characteristics

  • Annual probability of two adult individuals getting married/divorced, conditional on both individuals' characteristics.
  • Annual probability of two adult individuals sharing a household, conditional on both individuals' characteristics.
  • Annual probability of adding a child to the household, conditional on parent('s') characteristics.
  • Annual probability of child leaving the household, conditional on parent('s') and child's characteristics.

Education and employment

  • Annual probability of obtaining an educational degree (primary, secondary, tertriary), conditional on the individual's and parents' characteristics.
  • Annual probability of becoming unemployed conditional on the individual's characteristics.
  • Annual probability of early- or disability pension conditional on the individual's characteristics.
  • Annual probability of pension conditional on the individual's characteristics.

Income trajectories

  • Predicted lifetime income trajectory conditional on individual's characteristics.

Sick leave and benefits

  • Number of days of sickness and rehabilitation benefits conditional on specific NCD or MHC and the individual's other characteristics.
  • Amount paid and case management costs conditional on specific NCD or MHC and the individual's other characteristics.
