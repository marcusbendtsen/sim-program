SIMPRO

In this project, we will measure the reach and adoption of three digital behavioral interventions to study if and when dissemination can lead to implementation and at what cost. An additional aim of this study is to expand upon what is known about the acceptability of digital behavioral interventions.

"Individual characteristics" is broadly defined as what is know about an individual, e.g. age, gender, education, current income, household characteristics, NCDs, MHCs, history of sick leave, etc.

Study design

Three different interventions (Coach, Minska, and NEXit) will be made publicly available and advertised. Those who download the interventions will be asked to give baseline information on characteristics (age, gender, education, etc.) before using the intervention (and after informed consent).

Estimands

  • Reach: Annual probability of accessing a specific digital intervention conditional on the individual's characteristics.

  • Adoption:
    • Changes in known mediators of effects of a specific intervention, leading to attenuation of estimated conditional average effects of the intervention.
    • Microscopic engagement in intervention components associated with heterogenous effects, leading to attenuation of estimated conditonal average effects of the intervention.

  • Acceptability: Expand on what is know about acceptability of digital support instead of face-to-face among those looking for help online (qualitative study).
